Key Expressions

문장성분 Subject (S) Verb (V) Object (O) Complement (C) Modifier (M)
013 세금, 재정 ▼
AIt seems the government keeps adding new welfare programs, despite the recent tax cuts.
BIt won't be long before all this spending empties out our national coffers.
Simple
This spending will use up all of our nation's money.
Upgrade
This spending will empty out our national coffers.
014 세금, 재정 ▼
ADo you really think that more welfare without a tax hike is possible?
BFor the most part, yes. We can save a lot of money just by streamlining government expenditure.
Simple
We can save a lot of money just by saving government expenditure.
Upgrade
We can save a lot of money just by streamlining government expenditure.
015 세금, 재정 ▼
ADid you hear about those useless construction projects by the government that waste taxpayers' money?
BThe government is diminishing the soundness of the national budget by doing that.
Simple
The government is poorly managing the national budget.
Upgrade
The government is diminishing the soundness of the national budget.
016 세금, 재정 ▼
AI don't think individuals in the highest income bracket are paying the fair share of tax.
BIt 's not just those in the highest income bracket. We have tax evasion issues with people from every walk of life.
Simple
People who make more money are not paying the fair share of taxes.
Upgrade

017 세금, 재정 ▼
AThe public wants the government to improve our quality of life, but no one wants to pay for it.
BThe tax resistance we see is only there because our cost of living is already so high.
Simple
Many people oppose to tax increase because our cost of living is already so high.
Upgrade
The tax resistance is there because our cost of living is already so high.
018 세금, 재정 ▼
AIf we reduce taxes on large corporations, they 'll be inclined to invest more in the economy.

Simple
If we cut tax for large corporations, the money saved will be spent elsewhere.
Upgrade
If we give tax breaks to large corporations, the money saved will be spent elsewhere.
* tax breaks, tax benefits, tax credits: 세금 혜택
019 청년실업 ▼

The jobs available for those who have just graduated from college are very limited.
Upgrade
The jobs available for those fresh out of college are very limited.
020 청년실업 ▼

Simple

Upgrade

021 청년실업 ▼

Simple
Helping the service industry grow could be one way to create jobs.
Upgrade

Upgrade
Chaebol's reckless business expansion was one of the reasons for the financial crisis.
023 대기업 ▼
AThe Korean conglomerates have a very strong grip on the domestic market. I don't think they're ever going to fail at anything they do.

Simple

Upgrade

025 대기업 ▼

BI think the company deserves it. We should take this opportunity to root out such unfair business practices.

Upgrade
Consumers are pledging to boycott the company's products.
026 대기업 ▼
AThe company once again resorted to strong-arm tactics when selling its products to the franchises.
BThey should be punished severely this time so that similar unfair practices are never repeated.

Upgrade

027 대기업 ▼
AChaebols have their hands in every facet of Korean society. The government should expand the scope of industries that chaebols are banned from entering.
BI agree. If those corner stores are all dominated by big business, those small-sized store owners will have nowhere to go.
Simple
Chaebols are doing business in every sector.
Upgrade
Chaebols have their hands in every sector.
028 대기업 ▼
AThe large corporations are the reason for every problem we have in society.
BIf you keep demonizing them, you 'll never be able to address the underlying issues.
Simple
Stop describing large corporations too negatively.
Upgrade
Stop demonizing large corporations.
* demonize 악마로 묘사하다 (= vilify)
029 대기업 ▼
AIt 's been twenty years already since the 1997 financial crisis. I think Korea's economic system has gone through fundamental change.
BThere hasn't seemed to be much change in the way these conglomerates establish cozy ties with politicians.
Simple
Conglomerates set up close relationship with politicians.
Upgrade
Conglomerates establish cozy ties with politicians.

AWhat should we do to prevent bribery cases involving politicians and large business owners?
BMany people say we need more transparent corporate governance and more advanced corporate management.
Simple
Korean companies should be directed and controlled more transparently.
Upgrade
Korean companies need more transparent corporate governance.

Grammar

동명사의 역할 (Gerund as Subject, Object, Complement)

동명사는 문장에서 주어, 목적어, 보어 역할을 할 수 있습니다.

Running is the easiest way to lose weight.
달리기는 살을 빼는 가장 쉬운 방법이다.
동명사가 주어 역할
Writing a novel was one of my life goals.
소설을 쓰는 것은 내 인생 목표 중 하나였다.
동명사가 주어 역할
I like fishing and hunting.
나는 낚시와 사냥을 좋아한다.
동명사가 목적어 역할
She enjoys cooking dinner for her family.
그녀는 가족을 위해 저녁을 요리하는 것을 즐긴다.
동명사가 목적어 역할
My hobby is listening to music.
내 취미는 음악 듣기이다.
동명사가 보어 역할
One of his responsibilities is attending meetings.
그의 책임 중 하나는 회의에 참석하는 것이다.
동명사가 보어 역할

동명사의 특징

동명사는 동사의 성격을 그대로 가지므로 목적어를 가질 수 있고, 부사의 수식을 받을 수 있습니다.

I like reading a detective story.
reading + 목적어 a detective story
Being a nurse is very rewarding.
Being + 보어 a nurse
Running slowly is the best way to build endurance.
부사 slowly가 동명사 Running을 수식
We will get back to you after carefully reviewing your proposal.
부사 carefully가 동명사 reviewing을 수식

동명사 vs To부정사

일반적으로 동명사와 to부정사는 교환 가능하지만 (I like to cook = I like cooking), 뉘앙스 차이가 있습니다. 동명사는 현재 진행 중인 것, 일상적, 반복적인 행위를, to부정사는 미래의 것, 구체적(1회적) 행위를 나타냅니다.

동명사만 목적어로 취하는 동사:
admit, avoid, deny, dislike, enjoy, finish, give up, keep, mind, recommend, suggest, stop 등
